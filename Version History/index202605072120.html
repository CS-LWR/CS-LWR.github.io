About Me

I am interested in signal processing, electromagnetic forward and inverse modeling, magnetic induction sensing, and artificial intelligence for scientific computing.

My recent work focuses on frequency-domain electromagnetic modeling, layered-media inversion, voxel-based sensing structures, and AI-assisted reconstruction methods. I have also worked on EEG artifact removal using deep learning models such as convolutional autoencoders and variational autoencoders.

Research Interests

  • Magnetic induction sensing and FDEM inversion
  • Layered underground electromagnetic modeling
  • 3D forward modeling and voxelized inversion structures
  • Jacobian analysis, observability, and parameter cross-talk
  • AI-based signal reconstruction and EEG artifact removal
  • Wireless communication and digital signal processing

Selected Projects

Magnetic Induction Layered Inversion

This project studies frequency-domain magnetic induction sensing for underground layered media. The work includes transmitter-receiver coil modeling, secondary-field response analysis, Jacobian-based observability diagnosis, and constrained inversion.

Keywords: FDEM Magnetic Induction Layered Media Inverse Problem

3D Electromagnetic Forward Modeling

This project explores a stricter 3D forward-modeling route for electromagnetic sensing scenarios. The model considers practical transmitter and receiver positions, underground discretization, and spatially varying material properties.

Keywords: 3D Modeling Voxel Grid Forward Model Simulation

EEG Artifact Removal Using Deep Learning

This project investigates deep-learning-based EEG denoising methods, including convolutional autoencoders and variational autoencoders. The system evaluates reconstruction quality using metrics such as RRMSE, PSD-RRMSE, and Pearson correlation coefficient.

Keywords: EEG Autoencoder VAE Signal Denoising
